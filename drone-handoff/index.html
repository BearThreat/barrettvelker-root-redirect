Mr. Lawson Drone Handoff Guide

Started July 1, 2026. Use this as the operating checklist for the drone, radio, goggles, batteries, charger, and payload drop system.

Critical Safety Rules

  1. Treat the drone as dangerous any time the propellers are installed.
  2. Do not arm indoors.
  3. Do not plug in the flight battery unless the radio is already on, throttle is all the way down, and the arm switch is disarmed.
  4. Keep hands, wires, straps, clothing, and payload parts away from the propellers.
  5. Do not stand over the drone while it is powered.
  6. If anything looks wrong, land and disarm. Do not try to save a bad takeoff.
  7. GPS Rescue exists, but do not rely on it for normal operation. It is not DJI-style guaranteed return-to-home.

Radio / Stick Basics

  1. Open the radio.
  2. Put all switches into the upward position.
  3. Right stick centered in the middle.
  4. Left stick pushed all the way down.
  • Left stick is throttle and yaw.
  • Left stick up gives the drone more power and increases height.
  • Left stick left/right rotates the drone like a head swivel.
  • Right stick tilts the drone in the direction the stick is pushed.
  • Right stick controls forward, back, left, and right movement.

Normal Power-On Order

  1. Turn on the radio. The radio must be on before the drone is plugged in, or the radio may not connect.
  2. Take out the goggles.
  3. Turn on the goggles: press the bottom power button once, wait two seconds, then hold it for five seconds.
  4. Plug the flight battery into the drone.
  5. You should hear a small spark and a couple of beeps, then the radio should connect.
  6. Use only the yellow battery connector.
  7. Tuck the white connector between the wires so it cannot flop into the propellers.
  8. Put a twist in the battery cable so it stays stiff and away from the propeller blades.
  9. Wait for the goggles/video feed and radio connection to look normal before arming.

Switches / Modes

  • SA: arm/disarm. Only arm outdoors, in a clear area, with the drone sitting flat. Disarm immediately after landing.
  • SB: flight mode. Recommended handoff/basic flight mode is the middle position, Horizon. Horizon mode self-levels the drone to the horizon when the stick is released. Do not use the default/up Acro position for handoff/basic flights.
  • SF: beeper. Use to find the drone after landing.
  • SD: turtle / flip-over-after-crash. Do not use unless the drone is upside down and the propellers have clear space around them.

Payload Servo Remote

  • Payload release uses the dedicated servo remote and rotational servo from the previous drone setup.
  • Charge the servo receiver with USB-C.
  • Turn on the servo receiver by long-pressing the black button on the side.
  • Strap the servo drop receiver onto the side of the battery.
  • Do not strap the servo drop receiver to the bottom of the battery, because that can tip the drone over before flight.
  • Make sure the servo receiver cord cannot get wrapped in the propellers.
  • Turn on the servo receiver before loading the payload.
  • Press ON to engage the servo lock and hold the payload.
  • Press OFF to drop/release the payload.
  • Confirm ON is holding the payload before takeoff.
  • Keep the servo remote with the pilot or spotter during flight.

Payload / Drop Procedure

Known tested result: the drone successfully carried a 2.6 lb weight and dropped it in flight. One recorded example shows about a 2.4 lb metal basket payload held with bungee cords. It flew, but bouncing/swinging made the flight path worse than a rigid, centered mount would.

  1. Mount the payload tight, centered, and close to the frame.
  2. Do not use a swinging bag, long cord, or loose handle.
  3. Turn on the servo receiver.
  4. Press ON on the servo remote to engage the servo lock.
  5. Tug/check the payload gently and confirm it is actually held.
  6. Do a short hover first.
  7. If stable, fly to the drop location.
  8. Confirm the area below is clear.
  9. Press OFF on the servo remote to drop the payload.
  10. After the drop, expect the drone to feel lighter.
  11. Land and inspect the drone.

Preflight Checklist

  1. Open outdoor area.
  2. No people, cars, animals, or fragile things nearby.
  3. Battery is strapped tight and cannot slide.
  4. Payload is strapped tight, centered, close to the frame, and cannot swing.
  5. Prop nuts are tight.
  6. Wires and battery leads are twisted/tucked away from propellers.
  7. The white battery balance connector is tucked away from propellers.
  8. Goggles show video.
  9. Radio is connected.
  10. Throttle is all the way down.
  11. Arm switch is disarmed before plugging in the battery.
  12. If using a payload: servo receiver on, servo remote available, servo receiver strapped to the side of the battery, servo receiver cord secured away from props, press ON, and confirm the servo lock is holding the payload.

Takeoff / Basic Flying / Landing

Takeoff

  1. Set the drone on level ground.
  2. Stand back.
  3. Arm with SA.
  4. Slowly raise the left stick until the drone becomes light on the ground.
  5. If it tilts hard, wobbles, or sounds wrong, lower throttle, land, and disarm.
  6. For normal takeoff, lift to about waist height or lower until comfortable.
  7. Make small stick movements. This drone is powerful and does not hold position like a DJI camera drone.

Basic Flying

  • Use small, smooth inputs.
  • Left stick up/down controls height.
  • Left stick left/right rotates the drone.
  • Right stick moves the drone forward/back/left/right.
  • Do not do hard punch-outs or aggressive moves with a payload until repeat flights are boring.
  • If it starts drifting, gently correct with the right stick. Do not chase it with large inputs.

Landing / Shutdown

  1. Descend slowly.
  2. Touch down gently.
  3. Bring throttle all the way down.
  4. Disarm with SA.
  5. Unplug the drone battery.
  6. Turn off goggles.
  7. Turn off radio.
  8. Check all motors by touch. Warm is okay. Hot-to-touch means stop and do not fly again until inspected.
  9. Check battery, straps, wires, prop nuts, and payload mount.

Emergency Actions

  • If the drone wobbles badly: land and disarm.
  • If it leans hard on takeoff: lower throttle, land, disarm.
  • If it is drifting toward people or property: climb slightly if needed for clearance, steer away, then land.
  • If video looks bad before takeoff: do not arm.
  • If radio does not connect: unplug the drone battery, turn radio on first, then plug the drone battery back in.
  • If a propeller hits anything: disarm immediately and inspect before any further flight.
  • If the battery cable or white balance connector is loose near props: do not fly.

Battery Charging / Storage

Use the charger package and keep the battery adapters with the charger. The large battery terminals use adapters to shrink them down so they fit the charger.

  1. Plug in the cell voltage lead.
  2. Plug in the main positive and negative terminals using the charger adapters.
  3. Use channel selection until the correct battery screen is selected.
  4. Press the scroll wheel.
  5. Scroll all the way down.
  6. Select Start Task to begin charging.

Charging Current

  • Typical charge current is 1A to 3A.
  • 3A usually charges a battery from low to full in about 25-30 minutes.
  • Lower amperage is better for battery health and longevity, but takes longer.
  • The charger should automatically adapt to the battery type and charge each cell back up to 4.2V.

Runtime / Voltage

  • Current batteries cost about $45 each.
  • Expected flight time is about 4-5.5 minutes if not pushing the drone hard.
  • Short hard throttle bursts should be okay, but sustained heavy flying or heavy payloads drain faster.
  • Loose or swinging payloads drain battery and make control harder.
  • A double-capacity battery would cost around $120, but would not give exactly twice the flight time because the extra weight makes the drone work harder.
  • In theory, the drone should be able to handle roughly another 1.5 lb beyond the tested payload, but that would use battery faster and should be tested carefully.
  • Watch the voltage number in the bottom-left of the headset.
  • Land when it gets around 3.6V per cell.
  • 3.6V per cell is about as low as you should safely run the battery without risking semi-permanent internal battery damage.

Charging Safety

  • Do not leave charging unattended.
  • Charge on a nonflammable surface.
  • Stop using a battery if it is puffed, damaged, leaking, or smells wrong.
  • Let batteries cool before charging.
  • Store batteries at storage voltage when not flying again soon.
